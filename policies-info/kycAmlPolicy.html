AML (Anti-Money Laundering) Policy / Regulations / Procedures

 
  • The USA PATRIOT Act: This U.S. law requires financial institutions, including casinos and other gambling businesses, to establish anti-money laundering programs that include customer identification and verification procedures, ongoing monitoring of customer activity, and reporting of suspicious activity to the Financial Crimes Enforcement Network (FinCEN).
  • The Financial Action Task Force (FATF) Recommendations: The FATF is an intergovernmental organization that develops and promotes global AML and counter-terrorist financing (CFT) standards. The FATF Recommendations provide guidance on customer due diligence, record-keeping, and reporting of suspicious activity.
  • The European Union's Fourth Anti-Money Laundering Directive: This EU directive requires gambling businesses to implement AML policies and procedures, including customer due diligence, ongoing monitoring of customer activity, and reporting of suspicious activity to national financial intelligence units.
  • The United Kingdom's Gambling Commission: The UK's gambling regulator requires licensed gambling businesses to comply with AML regulations, including customer due diligence, ongoing monitoring of customer activity, and reporting of suspicious activity to the National Crime Agency.
 
To avoid its products and services being used for money laundering or terrorist financing, the company makes every effort to comply with strict AML and CTF regulations. Management and employees are also required to do the same. The implemented AML/CTF compliance program consists of, but is not limited to, the following policies and procedures:
  • Client identification and verification.
  • Ultimate Beneficial Owner identification and verification.
  • Identification of AML/CTF risks.
  • Effective implementation of AML/CTF policies and procedures to ensure compliance with AML laws and regulations.
  • Enhanced due diligence of high-risk clients.
  • Identification of clients, who qualify as Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs).
  • Internal procedures for monitoring and reporting suspicious activities of the client's.
  • Client screening against global list of terrorists and specially designated nationalities, relevant financial and other sanctions lists.
  • Staff training and awareness raising through specialized trainings and courses.
  • Maintenance of relevant records.
  • Management of regulatory inquiries and incidents.
  • Coordinating day-to-day compliance by responsible compliance staff.
 
 

KYC (Know Your Customer) Policy / Regulations / Procedures

 
The Company has created and enforces strict standards of KYC processes in order to maintain its commitment to ethical business practices and reduce any risks linked with money laundering, terrorist financing, and other illicit activities. These KYC policies/procedures cover a wide range of steps intended to confirm customers' identities, evaluate the source of their funds, continuously monitor transactions, and report any suspicious activity to the appropriate authorities as required by law. The Company seeks to foster openness, accountability, and confidence among its stakeholders while ensuring that its activities abide by all legal requirements by upholding these strict standards for KYC procedures. As a result, the Company's KYC policy and procedures are an essential component of its commitment to upholding a strong compliance culture and ethical business practices. The following actions are covered under these KYC policies and procedures:
  • Client's identification
  • Guidelines for accepting or rejecting clients (clients under age, clients from prohibited or sanctioned areas, etc.).
  • Monitoring of high-risk clients.
  • Gathering any data that can be crucial for assessing the AML risk.
  • When the relevant transaction has to be completed, KYC procedures are carried out.
  • The identified hazards are taken into consideration while KYC procedures are continually conducted.
 
 

Risk-Based Approach

 
When evaluating the risks associated with AML/CTF, the company uses a risk-based approach (RBA). The following are the company's rules:
 
  • To ensure that the client's identification does not match that of someone with a known criminal background, a banned entity, or a terrorist organization, the company conducts verification prior to any transaction.
  • Before beginning the relevant transaction, the client must provide the necessary information.
  • The process of determining a client's risk level may include evaluating the client's history, nation of origin, source of funds, and transaction volume.
  • When it comes to clients who may be deemed high-risk based on the organization's findings, the company may conduct increased due diligence.
  • When dealing with funds whose source is unknown or when dealing with more expensive or frequent transactions, the corporation may do greater due diligence.
 
 

Client Identification

 
The Client Identification Program is to be carried out:
 
  • prior to the completion of any financial transaction.
  • where there is any skepticism regarding the truthfulness, accuracy, or sufficiency of the previously acquired Clients' identity information.
  • under no circumstances does it allows any transaction to be performed with insufficient identity or, if necessary, residency verification information. It demands clients to present proof of identification and, if necessary, proof of residence.
 
 

Politically Exposed Persons

 
Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs) are subject to a special policy at the company.
The Company conducts PEP checks whenever it sees fit, at its own discretion.
The Company reserves the right to close the Account and return any existing deposits to the Player if the aforementioned checks reveal that the Player is a PEP. In this scenario, the Company reserves the right to charge the Player an administrative fee equal to 20% of the transferred funds.
 
 

Required Information

 
Clients go through an automated verification process where they submit:
  • Name
  • Email address
  • Date of birth
  • Copy of ID/Passport/Driver's license
  • Proof of residency (not more than six months old utility bills, bank statements, or similar documents).
 
 

Compliance Officer

 
The Compliance Officer, a firm employee, is in charge of effectively enforcing the AML/KYC Policy. The following are the responsibilities of the compliance officer:
 
  • Gathering identity data from customers.
  • Observation of the relevant transactions.
  • Defining and implementing internal policies and practices for the completion, evaluation, submission, and preservation of all reports and records as necessary to satisfy the demands of competent authorities.
  • Updating risk assessment.
  • Keeping up with AML records.
  • As required by the relevant laws and regulations, providing information to law enforcement.
  • The Compliance Officer has a duty to cooperate with competent law enforcement agencies engaged in the fight against money laundering, terrorist financing, and other criminal behavior.
  • Training of the staff.
